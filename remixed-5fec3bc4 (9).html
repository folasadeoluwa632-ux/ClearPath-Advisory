Frequently Asked
How long does LLC registration take?

Processing times vary by state, but most standard LLC formations complete within 3–7 business days. Expedited options are available in many states for 24-hour or same-day processing.

Do I need to be a U.S. citizen to form an LLC?

No. Non-U.S. residents and international entrepreneurs can legally form an LLC in the United States. We specialize in helping international business owners navigate this process.

What is a BOI filing and do I need one?

Beneficial Ownership Information filing is required under the Corporate Transparency Act for most U.S. businesses. It reports information about individuals who own or control your company to FinCEN.

What FDA registrations do you handle?

We handle food facility registration, cosmetic registration (MoCRA), medical device registration, drug establishment registration, FDA U.S. Agent services, product listing, and label compliance reviews.

Frequently Asked Questions

Find answers to the most common questions about our services.

How long does LLC formation take?

Standard processing ranges from 3 to 7 business days depending on the state. Expedited options are available in most states for 24-hour or same-day processing for an additional state fee.

Which state should I form my LLC in?

The best state depends on your situation. Wyoming and Delaware are popular for their business-friendly laws and strong legal frameworks, but forming in your home state is often more practical if you operate locally.

Can a non-U.S. resident form an LLC?

Yes. Non-U.S. residents and foreign nationals can legally form and own an LLC in the United States without a visa or social security number. We specialize in helping international entrepreneurs complete this process.

How long does it take to get an EIN?

For U.S. residents with an SSN, EIN can often be obtained same-day. For non-residents without an SSN or ITIN, the process typically takes 4 to 6 weeks through IRS mail correspondence.

What is FDA registration and who needs it?

FDA registration is required for any facility that manufactures, processes, packs, or holds food, drugs, medical devices, or cosmetics for U.S. consumers — including foreign facilities that export to the United States.

What is BOI filing and is it required?

BOI filing is required by FinCEN under the Corporate Transparency Act for most U.S. business entities. Non-compliance can result in significant civil and criminal penalties.
